METHODS OF SAVINGS

  • Bank Savings A/c:Opening a savings account at a bank is one of the simplest ways to save. They provide easy access to funds for emergencies or planned expenses with low or no fees.

    Advantages:

    • Safety and security:deposits in savings account are insured by the government and offer a guaranteed return in the form of interest with no risks involved.

    • Liquidity:It provides easy access to funds and withdraw money whenever needed which makes it the most suitable during an emergency.

    • Convenience:Savings accounts offer convenient features such as online banking, mobile banking and ATM access, making it easy for holders to make deposits and withdrawals from anywhere and anytime.

    Disadvantages:

    • Low Interest rates:the return on this method is often minimal due to low levels of interest rates. It is also eroded by inflation over time that decreases the real value of savings over time.

    • Opportunity cost:While savings accounts provide safety and liquidity, they may not offer the same growth potential as other methods that may be riskier but more rewarding over the long term Now that you know the pros and cons of a savings account, if you plan to go ahead with it, ensure you are aware of the interest rates offered by banks at present.

  • Fixed Deposits: It a type of savings account offered by banks and financial institutions where money is deposited for a fixed period at a predetermined interest rate.

    Advantages:

    • Safety and Security:With a guarantee of return, fixed deposits are insured up to a certain limit by government deposit insurance schemes, providing protection against loss in case of bank failure.

    • Fixed Returns:Fixed deposits offer a fixed rate of interest for the entire duration of the deposit, providing certainty and predictability of returns.

    • Flexibility in Tenure and interest payment:Fixed deposits offer flexibility in choosing the maturity period, ranging from a few months to several years. Investors can also choose between monthly, quarterly, or annual interest payouts.

    Disadvantages:

    • Lack of Liquidity:Unlike savings accounts, fixed deposits are not easily accessible before maturity without incurring penalties or loss of interest.

    • Low Interest rates:the return on this method is often minimal due to low levels of interest rates. It is also eroded by inflation over time that decreases the real value of savings over time.

    • Opportunity cost:While savings account provide safety and liquidity, they may not offer the same growth potential as other methods that may be riskier but more rewarding over the long term.
