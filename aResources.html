ADDITIONAL RESOURCES

Books

  • “Rich Dad Poor Dad” by Robert T. Kiyosaki- This book offers practical advice on building wealth and achieving financial independence by challenging conventional beliefs about money and investing.

  • "The Intelligent Investor" by Benjamin Graham- Considered a classic in the field of investing, this book provides timeless principles and strategies for value investing and building a successful investment portfolio.

  • "The Total Money Makeover" by Dave Ramsey- Dave Ramsey offers a step-by-step plan for getting out of debt, saving money, and building wealth through his proven principles of financial management.

  • "I Will Teach You to Be Rich" by Ramit Sethi- Ramit Sethi shares practical advice on managing money, automating finances, investing wisely, and achieving financial success without sacrificing the things you love.

  • "Your Money or Your Life" by Vicki Robin and Joe Dominguez- This book offers a holistic approach to personal finance, focusing on achieving financial independence and aligning your spending with your values and life goals.

  • "The Little Book of Common Sense Investing" by John C. Bogle- John Bogle, the founder of Vanguard Group, advocates for a simple and low-cost approach to investing through index funds, emphasizing the importance of long-term investing and minimizing fees.

  • "Broke Millennial: Stop Scraping By and Get Your Financial Life Together" by Erin Lowry- Geared towards millennials, this book offers practical advice on managing money, paying off debt, investing, and achieving financial independence.

  • "The Automatic Millionaire" by David Bach- David Bach introduces the concept of "paying yourself first" and shares strategies for automating your finances, saving money, and building wealth over time.

  • "The Richest Man in Babylon" by George S. Clason- This book offers timeless financial lessons through parables set in ancient Babylon, covering topics such as saving, investing, budgeting, and building wealth.

  • "Money: Master the Game" by Tony Robbins: Tony Robbins explores the world of finance and investing, offering insights from top financial experts and providing practical advice for achieving financial freedom and security.

Movies

  • The Big Short (2015): Based on the true story of the 2008 financial crisis, this film follows a group of investors who predicted the housing market collapse and bet against the banks. It provides insight into the complexities of the financial system and the risks associated with investing.

  • Margin Call (2011): Set during the early stages of the 2008 financial crisis, this thriller follows employees at an investment bank as they uncover unethical practices and make decisions that will impact the global economy. It offers a behind-the-scenes look at the world of high finance and the moral dilemmas faced by those working in the industry.

  • Wall Street (1987): Directed by Oliver Stone, this classic film stars Michael Douglas as Gordon Gekko, a ruthless and greedy Wall Street investor. It explores themes of corporate greed, insider trading, and the moral compromises made in the pursuit of wealth and power.

  • Moneyball (2011): Based on a true story, this sports drama follows Oakland Athletics general manager Billy Beane (played by Brad Pitt) as he uses data analysis and unconventional strategies to build a competitive baseball team on a limited budget. It highlights the importance of analytics, innovation, and resourcefulness in achieving success.

  • The Pursuit of Happyness (2006): Inspired by the true story of Chris Gardner, this biographical drama stars Will Smith as a struggling salesman who faces homelessness while pursuing a career as a stockbroker. It emphasizes the importance of perseverance, hard work, and determination in overcoming adversity and achieving financial stability.

  • Inside Job (2010): This documentary film provides a comprehensive analysis of the 2008 financial crisis, examining the systemic flaws and regulatory failures that led to the collapse of the global economy. It offers insights from economists, policymakers, and industry insiders on the root causes of the crisis and the lessons that can be learned from it.

  • The Wolf of Wall Street (2013): Directed by Martin Scorsese and starring Leonardo DiCaprio, this biographical black comedy follows the rise and fall of Jordan Belfort, a stockbroker who engaged in fraudulent activities and lived a lavish lifestyle. It offers a cautionary tale about the dangers of greed, excess, and unethical behavior in the world of finance.

  • It's a Wonderful Life (1946): Although not explicitly about finance, this classic Christmas film explores themes of financial hardship, community support, and the impact of individual actions on others. It reminds viewers of the importance of gratitude, generosity, and the true meaning of wealth beyond material possessions.

Government Schemes

  • Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY): This scheme aims to provide universal access to banking facilities to all households in India. It offers a basic savings bank deposit account, RuPay debit card, and overdraft facility.

  • Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (PMMY): This scheme provides loans to micro-enterprises from non-corporate, non-farm sectors. It has three sub-schemes: Shishu (loan up to Rs. 50,000), Kishore (loan between Rs. 50,000 and Rs. 5 lakh), and Tarun (loan between Rs. 5 lakh and Rs. 10 lakh).

  • Stand Up India Scheme: This scheme aims to promote entrepreneurship among women and Scheduled Castes (SCs) and Scheduled Tribes (STs) by facilitating bank loans up to Rs. 1 crore.

  • Atal Pension Yojana (APY): This scheme is a voluntary pension scheme for people in the unorganized sector. The government co-contributes up to Rs. 1,000 per year to the subscriber's account.

  • Pradhan Mantri Jeevan Jyoti Bima Yojana (PMJJBY) and Pradhan Mantri Suraksha Bima Yojana (PMSBY): These are two social security schemes that offer life insurance and accidental death and disability cover, respectively, at a very low premium.
