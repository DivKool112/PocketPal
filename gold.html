GOLD

Investing in gold can be a strategic addition to an investment portfolio for diversification and hedging against economic uncertainty.

Physical Gold: Buying physical gold in the form of bullion bars, coins, or jewelry is a common way to invest in gold.

Advantages

  • Tangible asset:Tangible asset: Physical gold provides ownership of a tangible asset that can be held directly by the investor.

  • Safe haven: Gold is often considered a safe haven asset that can preserve wealth during times of economic instability or inflation.

Disadvantages

  • Physical gold requires secure storage facilities to protect against theft or loss, which may incur additional costs

  • Liquidity: Selling physical gold may involve transaction costs such as making and melting charges. It may not be as liquid as other forms of gold investment.

  • Investment will have to be made at the standard price in the market and cannot be made with small amounts like in ETFs.

Gold Exchange Traded-Funds (ETFs): Gold ETFs are exchange-traded funds that invest in physical gold bullion and aim to track the price of gold.

Advantages

  • Diversification: Gold ETFs offer exposure to gold prices without the need for physical ownership, providing diversification benefits to investment portfolios.

  • Accessibility: Gold ETFs can be bought and sold on stock exchanges like individual stocks, providing liquidity and ease of trading.

  • Liquidity

Disadvantages

Counterparty risk: Gold ETFs are subject to counterparty risk associated with the custodian holding the physical gold.

Gold Mutual Funds: Gold mutual funds invest in shares of companies engaged in gold mining, exploration, or production.

Advantages

  • Indirect exposure:Gold mutual funds provide indirect exposure to gold prices through investments in gold mining stocks, offering potential for capital appreciation.

  • Professional management Gold mutual funds are managed by professional portfolio managers who conduct research and make investment decisions on behalf of investors.

Disadvantages

  • Equite risk: Gold mutual funds are subject to equity market risks, including company-specific risks, industry risks, and market volatility.

  • Performance variability:he performance of gold mutual funds may not perfectly track the price of gold due to factors such as company performance, operational issues, or management decisions.

Sovereign Gold Bonds: Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGBs) are government securities denominated in grams of gold issued by the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) on behalf of the Government of India. These bonds have tenure of eight years and when held till the maturity date benefit the investors without having to pay any taxes. However, in case of emergency, these bonds can be sold on stock exchange to retrieve the investment but the this move becomes open to taxes.

Advantages

  • Safety and Security Sovereign Gold Bonds are backed by the Government of India, providing investors with a high level of safety and security compared to other forms of gold investment.

  • Intrest income In addition to the potential for capital appreciation based on changes in the price of gold, Sovereign Gold Bonds offer a fixed rate of interest on the initial investment amount. The interest rate is paid semi-annually and is linked to the prevailing market rates.

  • Capital Gains Tax Benefits: Sovereign Gold Bonds offer capital gains tax benefits if held until maturity. Capital gains arising from the redemption or transfer of SGBs are exempt from capital gains tax if held until maturity.

  • Liquidity Sovereign Gold Bonds can be traded on stock exchanges within a specified period after the lock-in period, providing liquidity and flexibility to investors who wish to exit their investments before maturity.

Disadvantages

  • Fixed Lock-In Period Sovereign Gold Bonds have a fixed lock-in period of five years from the date of issuance, during which investors cannot redeem or transfer their bonds.

  • Price Volatility The price of Sovereign Gold Bonds is linked to the prevailing market prices of gold and may fluctuate based on changes in gold prices, currency exchange rates, and other market factors, leading to potential price volatility.
