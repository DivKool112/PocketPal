STOCKS

Stock Market Investing: Investing in stocks involves purchasing shares of ownership in publicly traded companies.

Advantages

  • Potential for High Returns: Stocks can provide higher average returns compared to other investment methods such as FDs when invested in the right companies over the long term.

  • Diversification: Stock market investing allows investors to diversify their portfolios across different industries, sectors, and geographic regions. Diversification helps spread risk and minimise the impact of poor performance in any single stock or sector on the overall portfolio.

  • Liquidity: Stocks are highly liquid investments, meaning they can be bought or sold quickly on public stock exchanges during trading hours. Dividend Income: Many publicly traded companies distribute a portion of their profits to shareholders in the form of dividends.

Disadvantages

  • Market Volatility: Prices fluctuate daily in response to various factors such as economic conditions, corporate earnings, geopolitical events, and investor sentiment. Market volatility can lead to significant short-term price swings and uncertainty, potentially causing panic among investors.

  • Risk of Loss: Investing in stocks carries the risk of losing capital, especially in the short term. Stock prices can be influenced by unpredictable factors, including market speculation, company-specific issues, regulatory changes, and external shocks.

  • Emotional Investing: Stock market investing can evoke emotions such as fear, greed, and overconfidence, leading to impulsive decision-making and irrational behavior.
